Right at Camp: Tieton River

Good for wading and cooling off — not for serious swimming or kayaking in July.

Windy Point sits right on the Tieton, and campers describe shallow shoreline areas near the sites where kids can safely wade and splash. But July isn't the river's real paddling season — the Tieton's famous whitewater comes from a scheduled dam release out of Rimrock Reservoir that happens every September (the "flip-flop"), so in July the flow is lower and more suited to wading than kayaking. The water also runs cold even in summer heat.

Good for: wading, splashing, cooling off feet, casual play right outside your tent. Not ideal for: swimming laps or bringing kayaks — current and cold water make it more of a dip-your-toes spot with young kids around.

Best Bet: Rimrock Lake

~20–25 min west on Highway 12 toward White Pass — calm water, sandy swim area, kayak rentals on site.

Calmest Option: Clear Lake

Just past Rimrock, same direction — non-motorized only, small and calm.

Water Play Packing

If renting kayaks at Silver Beach, worth calling ahead in July to confirm availability for a group your size — popular spot in peak season.

Second Vehicle — No Guaranteed Lot

Windy Point is a small 15-site campground. There's no confirmed overflow lot — extra vehicles are space-permitting within your own site.
